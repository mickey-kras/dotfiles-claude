# Executive Summary Playbook

## When to produce

- when a full report needs to be presented to leadership
- when time-constrained stakeholders need key takeaways
- when a decision meeting needs a briefing document

## Structure

1. **Situation** (2-3 sentences): what was investigated and why
2. **Key findings** (3-5 numbered items): most important discoveries
3. **Recommended actions** (prioritized): what to do next
4. **Risks and open questions**: what could change the conclusions
5. **Confidence statement**: overall confidence level and what would change it

## Rules

- the summary must stand alone without requiring the full report
- every finding in the summary must appear in the source report
- do not introduce new claims or recommendations not in the source
- keep total length under 500 words
- use numbered items for easy reference in discussion
- highlight decisions that need to be made, not just information
