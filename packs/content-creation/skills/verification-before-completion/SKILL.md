# Verification Before Completion

Use this skill before calling content work done.

## Goal-backward verification

A draft that exists is not a draft that satisfies the brief. Verify backward:

1. What must be TRUE for the brief to be satisfied?
2. What must EXIST as deliverables for those truths to hold?
3. Do the deliverables match the audience, channel, and message hierarchy?

## Verification steps

- confirm the brief, audience, and channel requirements still match the draft
- verify claims, citations, and asset provenance notes
- check that requested deliverables exist, not just summaries of them
- note any intentional deviation from the original brief
- re-read the original ask and compare it to what was delivered
- if the delivered output intentionally differs from the brief, document why

## Evidence before claims

Do not say "the campaign is ready" without verifying:
- all requested deliverables exist as files, not descriptions
- citations and claims pass the claims-verification rule
- brand consistency has been checked
- channel adaptations match the channel plan
